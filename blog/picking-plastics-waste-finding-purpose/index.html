Picking Plastics Waste, Finding Purpose

📅 Published on: August 16, 2025

✍️ Written by: Caleb Chikanka Wodi

Today was supposed to be just another day of picking plastic waste, but it turned out to be a defining moment in my journey.

As I went out to collect sachet water nylon waste from gutter to gutter, I didn’t let the potential negativity affect me. The day started like any other. I wore my gloves and setting out to pick plastic waste.

People passing and were wondering what I was doing. While they saw only the picking, I saw the recycling.

As I picked for hours, an unforgettable incident occurred. Approaching the main road, I was confronted by the security guards.

They asked where I was taking the waste, and I replied that I was taking it to a recycling company.

For some reason, this didn’t sit well with them. They told me to kneel down and even threatened to flog me with a cutlass if I didn’t comply.

I was taken aback by their aggression, and I refused to kneel, unsure of what I had done wrong. The situation escalated quickly, with the guards dragging me, punching me, and pushing me into the gutter.

Just when things seemed like they couldn’t get any worse, a lady intervened, asking me to explain what was happening.

In the midst of the chaos, my dad arrived. His presence diffused the situation. He explained to the guards that I was his son and that we were residents of the estate.

I apologized to everyone, and the guards apologized to me. We parted ways.

Reflecting on the incident, I realized the guards saw me as an outsider, someone who didn’t belong in the estate. But my dad’s intervention revealed that I was, in fact, part of the community.

I learned a valuable lesson about introducing myself and establishing my identity in situations like these.

Despite the setbacks, I continued my mission to collect plastic waste, driven by my passion for recycling. Looking at the waste scattered around, I didn’t just see trash but I saw purpose. I saw an opportunity to make a difference, to contribute to a cleaner environment, and to inspire others to do the same.

This experience taught me that success is not about what others think of me, but about what I want for myself. It’s about persevering in the face of adversity and staying true to my values.

As I move forward, I’ll carry this lesson with me and I’ll continue to pick up where others might leave off.

The Takeaway

Sometimes, it’s not about the destination; it’s about the journey.

Today, I encountered a challenge that tested my resolve, but it also gave me a newfound appreciation for the importance of community and identity.

Every piece of plastic waste I collect brings me closer to my goal of making a difference.
